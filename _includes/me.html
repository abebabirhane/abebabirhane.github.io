I am a cognitive science PhD researcher at the Complex Software Lab in the school of computer scienceat University college Dublin, Ireland. My interdisciplinary research sits at the intersections of complex adaptive systems, machine learning, and critical race studies. On the one hand, complexity science tells us that people, as complex adaptive systems are inherently indeterminable. On the other, machine learning systems that claim to predict human behaviour are becoming ubiquitous in all spheres of social life. Machine prediction, when deployed to high-stake situations, not only is erroneous but also presents real harm to those at the margins of society. I examine questions of such nature in my PhD. I organize the data economies and data governance working group, one of the MD4SGworking groups. Starting from January 2020, I take the position of Research Scientist Intern at DeepMind. I have other numerous ongoing projects and I look forward to sharing them when they are complete. However, due to excessive ongoing projects and an upcoming full-time Research Scientist Intern position at DeepMind, I am unable to accept invitations to speak at conferences, workshops, and other events.
